# Employee Advocacy Platform (EMP-ADVOCACY): questions waiting for you

## What this domain is
Turn your employees into your most trusted social channel.

Curate approved, on-brand content and broadcast it to your people, who reshare it to their own
LinkedIn, X, and Facebook networks in one click with copy they can personalize. Reward
participation with points, badges, and leaderboards, surface your top advocates, send each person
a tailored newsletter of what is worth sharing, and measure the clicks, reach, and earned media
value every share drives, then tie that activity to the pipeline it influences.

q1: (answer this first) Should Employee Advocacy stay its own domain, or fold into Social Media Management?

- a) Keep it as its own domain, Employee Advocacy (this is how it was built).
- b) Fold it into Social Media Management's influencer/creator area and remove the separate domain.

Recommended: a. There is a real, independent market here. DSMN8, GaggleAMP, and EveryoneSocial are
products whose whole reason for existing is employee advocacy (DSMN8 and GaggleAMP are independent;
EveryoneSocial is now owned by Seismic but still sold as a standalone advocacy product), with
Oktopost and Sociabble alongside. Gartner even lists a dedicated "Employee Advocacy Tools" market.
Social Media Management is about your brand's own channels and external influencers (it already has
a separate influencer module), and it does not model any of what advocacy needs: advocate profiles,
their linked personal accounts, employee shares, leaderboards, or share-to-pipeline attribution. It
also has a different buyer, marketing and communications run it, with HR helping on the
employer-brand side. Choice (b) would lose all of that structure.

q2: Approve the workflow behaviors I set on a few records?

I set three things during the build. (1) Published advocacy content is frozen and goes through one
approver, so a share always points at an approved version. (2) An advocate's profile and (3) their
linked personal social accounts are treated as personal data (GDPR), so retention and erasure rules
apply.

- Approve all
- Approve some (tell me which)
- Reject all

Recommended: approve all. Advocacy platforms freeze published content and route it through a single
content owner so a reshare pins to an approved revision, and the advocate's identity plus their
connected LinkedIn/X/Facebook accounts are clearly personal data across every flagship vendor.

q3: Which compliance frameworks should be attached to Employee Advocacy?

- a) GDPR only.
- b) GDPR plus FINRA (only if you run advocacy in financial services, where employee social posts
  are supervised).
- c) None.

Recommended: a. Advocacy is not a regulation-mandated market, but every platform stores advocate
contact details and personal social-account connections, so GDPR is always load-bearing. FINRA-style
social supervision only applies if you are a regulated financial-services firm, so it is the
conditional add, not the default.
