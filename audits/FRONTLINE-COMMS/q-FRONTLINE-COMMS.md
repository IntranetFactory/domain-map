# Frontline / Deskless Worker Communication (FRONTLINE-COMMS): questions waiting for you

## What this domain is
Reach, task, and recognize your frontline workforce from one mobile app, no corporate email required.

Connect every deskless worker on the floor through a branded mobile app, kiosk, or shared device. Broadcast targeted announcements and confirm who has read them, push tasks and checklists that workers complete and submit from their phones, and let teams swap shifts, finish bite-size training, and recognize each other in the same place. Built for store, plant, warehouse, and field staff who never had a work email, with handoff back to scheduling and store operations.

> Grounding: these recommendations are backed by a fresh vendor-surface study (5 independent flagship pure-plays + suite-absorption M&A, 2025-2026) saved at `.tmp_deploy/FRONTLINE-COMMS-phase0-2026-06-14.md`. The domain was built live at `record_status='new'`; the two questions below are the market-shape confirmation and one destructive re-shelving step that need your sign-off.

---

q1: (answer this first) Should frontline / deskless worker communication stand as its own domain, or fold into the existing Employee Communications and Intranet domain?

- a) Keep it as its own domain (FRONTLINE-COMMS, already built: 5 capabilities, 2 modules, 10 masters)
- b) Fold it into Employee Communications and Intranet and delete the new domain

Recommended: a. Five independent pure-plays sell the unified frontline platform as one flagship product, and none of the adjacent domains fuses what they fuse. WorkJam packages comms + task + scheduling + shift swap + training + recognition in one mobile app; YOOBIC packages comms + task and operations execution + microlearning; Speakap, Flip, and Blink each sell a white-label frontline app spanning broadcast, chat, shifts, and recognition. By contrast Employee Communications and Intranet is an office-comms publishing platform bought by Internal Comms or HR (its "frontline" capability is reach extension, not task execution or shift conversations); Workforce Management owns the shift record and swap approval, not the worker-facing swap conversation; Retail Store Operations owns retail-only task execution; Employee Experience owns survey listening, not broadcast or recognition. The buyer is different too (store and plant operations, for workers with no corporate email). WorkJam is already in the catalog described as a "Frontline digital workplace, communications, and WFM" but mis-shelved on Workforce Management and Retail Store Operations, which is itself evidence the catalog needs this home. Suites are buying into the category (Beekeeper merged with LumApps in 2025, Workvivo into Zoom, Crew into Square), not collapsing it.

q2: Should the existing WorkJam product (currently filed under Workforce Management and Retail Store Operations) also be filed under this new frontline domain as its primary market?

- a) Add a primary frontline-domain link, keep its existing Workforce Management and Retail Store Operations links
- b) Add a primary frontline-domain link and downgrade the Workforce Management and Retail Store Operations links to secondary
- c) Leave WorkJam where it is

Recommended: a. WorkJam's flagship is the frontline digital workplace (the same product YOOBIC, Speakap, Flip, and Blink compete with), so its primary market is this new domain and Workforce Management and store operations are adjacencies it also touches. Option (a) is purely additive (a new coverage link, nothing overwritten) and is safe to apply now. Option (b) re-points two existing links from primary to secondary, which is a destructive overwrite and is the only reason this is a question rather than an automatic fix; pick (b) only if you want the catalog to assert that frontline is WorkJam's primary and the other two are secondary. The intranet-led vendors already on Employee Communications and Intranet (Staffbase, Simpplr, Firstup) stay where they are; they are comms-led, not ops-led frontline platforms.
